Things That Interest Me

Volunteer Work

Garden construction and rehabilitation with Portland Community Gardens

Learn more about community gardens

In 2007, I began volunteering with the city of Portland's Community Gardens as a project leader. With only a small staff and 50+ gardens to maintain, they need all the help they can get!

I coordinated work crews to help with a variety of projects ranging from site cleanup to invasive species removal to garden construction. My favorite part is when I get a call afterwards from a volunteer telling me that they enjoyed it so much that they now have their own garden plot!

Keeping film history alive with the Hollywood Theatre

Learn more about the Hollywood Theatre

Built in 1926, the Hollywood is one of Portland's longest-running movie theaters and a cultural and architectural treasure. Believe it or not, it's a 501(c)3 nonprofit too! Volunteers help out with special events and educational programming as well as selling tickets and concessions. This helps them keep their staffing costs down so they can put more resources into preserving the building and supporting film preservation and educational programming. I used to train new volunteers - these days I mostly just pop in for shifts occasionally where I pour beers, sneak handfuls of popcorn, and talk about movies.

Musical adventures with the Portland Old Time Music Gathering

Learn more about the Gathering

(Unofficial slogan: "Unattended children will be given a box of doughnuts and an out of tune banjo.") The Gathering was started by the late Bill Martin as a way of preserving this old (but not forgotten) style of music and dance. I'm on the organizing committee, serving as the volunteer coordinator and box office manager. We work year-round to put on an annual event each MLK Day weekend. It brings together a few hundred people each year for a variety of classes, dances, concerts, jam sessions and musical fellowship.

Pssst! If you're looking for places to get involved in town, especially if you're new to town and want to get to know some awesome people, here are some suggestions:

  1. Hands On Greater Portland: They have a calendar with many done-in-a-day volunteer events you can sign up for. Or, you can get information on organizations that need ongoing volunteer help. Want to play with cute animals? Paint schools? Plant trees? Make greeting cards for housebound seniors? Serve meals at a shelter? They can hook you up with all of these things... and more!

  2. Free Geek: Learn to build your own computer, benefit the environment, gain job skills, or just come to have fun!

  3. Rebuilding Center: They offer affordable used building and remodeling materials with the goal of promoting the use of salvaged and reclaimed materials, and they have lots of different volunteer opportunities (and some great classes too!)

Last Regiment of Syncopated Drummers

Learn more about LRSD

In January 2014, I started training with LRSD. I began performing with them on cymbals that summer, and am now cross-training on a tenor drum. Sometimes we get to travel to events like the San Francisco Pride Parade or Bumbershoot, but mostly we play at events around the Portland metro area. If you're a runner you may have heard us at the start of the Portland marathon.
